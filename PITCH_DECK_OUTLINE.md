# Pitch Deck Outline

**Product:** FirstTake by MVA Studios  
**Platform:** Nata Connect  
**Tagline:** Where Talent Meets Opportunity.

This is a 10-slide founder pitch deck outline. It is designed for early Talent
users, recruiters, mentors, college reviewers, incubators, investors, and demo
audiences. Keep the deck honest: no fake traction, no guaranteed casting, no
unstated partnerships, no enterprise-scale claims, and no promises around AI,
payments, or video upload.

## Slide 1: Title

Objective:

Introduce the product clearly and set the emotional frame.

Key message:

FirstTake by MVA Studios is building Nata Connect, a trusted casting workflow
for Talent and verified recruiters.

Suggested visual:

Landing page hero from `/` with the brand, tagline, and primary CTA visible.

Speaker note:

"This is FirstTake by MVA Studios. Our platform is Nata Connect, built around
one promise: Where Talent Meets Opportunity. The goal is to make casting more
organized, safer, and easier to trust for both Talent and casting teams."

## Slide 2: Problem

Objective:

Make the audience feel the real workflow pain before showing the product.

Key message:

Audition discovery and applicant review are fragmented across social media,
informal chats, spreadsheets, and unclear forms.

Suggested visual:

Simple diagram showing scattered channels: Instagram posts, WhatsApp chats,
Google Forms, spreadsheets, informal referrals.

Speaker note:

"The issue is not that Talent lack ambition or that recruiters lack judgment.
The issue is workflow fragmentation. Talent do not always know what is real,
what to submit, or where their application stands. Recruiters often lose
context while reviewing applicants across multiple disconnected channels."

## Slide 3: Target Users

Objective:

Clarify who the product serves first.

Key message:

Nata Connect starts with emerging Talent and verified casting teams, with a
Telugu/Tollywood-first focus for a controlled early launch.

Suggested visual:

Two-column user map:

- Talent: actors, models, dancers, anchors, voice artists
- Recruiters: casting directors, producers, agencies, studios, student film
  teams

Speaker note:

"The first users are emerging performers who need visibility and serious
casting teams who need structured applicant review. We are starting focused
rather than broad because trust and early feedback matter more than volume at
this stage."

## Slide 4: Solution

Objective:

Show the core platform concept in one sentence and one workflow.

Key message:

Nata Connect turns casting into one connected loop: profile, audition,
application, review, decision, communication, and trust operations.

Suggested visual:

Product loop graphic:

Talent profile -> Audition discovery -> Application pack -> Recruiter review
-> Messages and notifications -> Admin trust layer

Speaker note:

"The product is not trying to automate casting decisions. It creates the
workflow layer around casting decisions. Talent present themselves
professionally, recruiters publish clearer briefs, applications stay trackable,
and admins can review trust and safety activity."

## Slide 5: Product Walkthrough

Objective:

Make the MVP feel tangible and working.

Key message:

The product already supports the central marketplace workflow across Talent,
Recruiter, and Admin roles.

Suggested visual:

Four-screen collage:

- `/talent/profile`
- `/auditions`
- `/applications`
- `/recruiter/auditions/[id]/applicants`

Speaker note:

"For Talent, the workflow starts with profile readiness, then audition
discovery and applications. For recruiters, it starts with company verification,
then casting brief creation and applicant review. For admins, it is verification,
reports, moderation, and launch readiness."

## Slide 6: Trust and Safety

Objective:

Position trust as a product system, not a marketing line.

Key message:

Trust is built into the product through verification, admin review, reports,
audit logs, application-linked messaging, and safety guidance.

Suggested visual:

Admin dashboard or `/admin/beta-readiness` screenshot plus small badges:
Verified recruiter, Talent verification, Reports, Audit logs, Never pay to
audition.

Speaker note:

"Casting is trust-sensitive. This MVP includes manual recruiter verification,
optional Talent verification, private evidence review, reports, audit logs, and
safe messaging rules. It does not remove every risk, but it creates a clearer
review trail than informal channels."

## Slide 7: Why Telugu / Tollywood First

Objective:

Explain the market wedge without overclaiming market size.

Key message:

A Telugu/Tollywood-first launch gives the product cultural clarity, a reachable
early audience, and a practical way to learn before expanding.

Suggested visual:

Regional-first positioning map or simple three-point visual:

- Active creative ecosystem
- Mobile-first discovery behavior
- Trust-sensitive audition process

Speaker note:

"Regional-first is a strategic wedge. Telugu cinema, student films,
independent creators, advertising, and local production work create real demand
for new Talent. The market is relationship-driven and mobile-first, which makes
clarity and trust especially valuable."

## Slide 8: MVP Progress and Current Status

Objective:

Show progress without claiming traction that does not exist.

Key message:

The working MVP has been smoke-tested across public, Talent, Recruiter, Admin,
API, Firestore, and Storage flows and is ready for controlled beta demos.

Suggested visual:

Progress checklist:

- Auth and role routing
- Talent profiles and media
- Audition discovery
- Application tracker
- Recruiter applicant review
- Messaging and notifications
- Admin trust operations
- Launch readiness command center
- Lint, tests, build passing

Speaker note:

"This is not just a mockup. The app has working role-based flows, Firebase
Auth, Firestore, Storage, Admin route handlers, security rules, and automated
checks. The right next milestone is not a broad launch. It is a controlled
beta with real users and close operations."

## Slide 9: Beta Launch Plan

Objective:

Show a realistic operating plan.

Key message:

The first beta should be small, founder-led, and measured around activation,
trust, and workflow clarity.

Suggested visual:

30-day beta timeline:

Week 1: invite 10-20 Talent and 2-5 trusted recruiters  
Week 2: collect applications and recruiter feedback  
Week 3: review reports, verification, and friction  
Week 4: fix blockers and decide expansion criteria

Speaker note:

"The plan is intentionally small. We invite a controlled group, monitor profile
completion, auditions posted, applications submitted, recruiter response time,
reports, and trust issues. We expand only after the core loop behaves well."

## Slide 10: Ask / Next Step

Objective:

Make the next action clear for the audience.

Key message:

Help FirstTake validate the controlled beta with real Talent, trusted
recruiters, mentorship, and operating discipline.

Suggested visual:

Three ask cards:

- Introduce trusted beta Talent
- Introduce legitimate casting teams
- Support product review, mentorship, or incubation

Speaker note:

"The ask depends on the audience. For Talent, join the beta and complete a real
profile. For recruiters, post one clear casting brief after verification. For
mentors and incubators, help us run the first beta responsibly and measure the
right signals before scaling."

## Deck Integrity Checklist

- Do not say "guaranteed jobs" or "guaranteed casting."
- Do not claim thousands of users or fake traction.
- Do not claim partnerships unless they are real and approved to mention.
- Do not imply enterprise-scale readiness.
- Do not imply AI matching, payments, video calls, or direct video upload exist.
- Say self-tapes are external links only.
- Say the next milestone is controlled beta, not broad public launch.
